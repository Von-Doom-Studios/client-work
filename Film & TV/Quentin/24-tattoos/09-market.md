# MARKET POSITIONING & WHY NOW

---

## The Audience

*24 Tattoos* targets the same audience that made *Knives Out* a $311M global hit and drove *The Intouchables* to become one of the highest-grossing French films ever released internationally — adults 25–50 who want to laugh, feel something real, and leave the theater with something to talk about.

It is not a niche arthouse film. It is not a franchise tentpole. It's a precision-engineered crowd-pleaser with genuine artistic integrity — the kind of film that works at festivals, works on streaming, and works at the box office.

---

## Why Now

**The gig economy is the new everyman story.**
Pierre on his Uber Eats bike is a protagonist audiences recognize instinctively. The struggling creative, the invisible worker, the person doing everything right and going nowhere — that character resonates globally in 2026 in a way it never has before. He's not a loser. He's overqualified for his circumstances.

**Crypto has cultural fluency.**
The seed phrase as a plot device is immediately legible to a broad audience in a way it wouldn't have been a decade ago. The film doesn't explain blockchain — it uses the logic of it (decentralized, permanent, trust-based) as thematic architecture. Audiences get it, and it feels current.

**The market wants smart commercial comedies.**
*Glass Onion*, *Bodies Bodies Bodies*, *The Banshees of Inisherin*, *Parasite* — the last several years have proven that audiences are hungry for films that respect their intelligence and still deliver genre satisfaction. *24 Tattoos* fits this lane precisely.

**The donation ending is the differentiator.**
Most caper films end with the money. This one doesn't. That choice — which is entirely earned by the script — makes the film memorable in a way the genre rarely is. It's the kind of ending that gets shared. That gets discussed. That travels.

---

## Positioning

> **The film that *Knives Out* would make if it were set in Paris, written by someone who's actually been broke.**

---

## Format & Release Strategy

- **Format:** Feature film, approximately 100 minutes
- **Primary market:** English-language streaming (A24, Netflix, Prime, Apple TV+) and theatrical
- **Festival strategy:** Sundance, Toronto, Tribeca — strong fit for narrative competition and audience award tracks
- **International:** French-language theatrical natural; European co-production eligible
