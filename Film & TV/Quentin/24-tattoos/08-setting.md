# SETTING

---

## Paris — Two Cities in One

The film is grounded in a Paris of sharp contrasts, and those contrasts are the visual language of Pierre's world:

**Pierre's Paris:** Cramped studio apartments. Wet asphalt at 2am. Casting rooms with fluorescent lighting. Uber Eats routes through arrondissements he'll never afford to live in. The city as an obstacle course for the broke and ambitious.

**Olivier's Paris:** Cathedral ceilings. Carrara marble consoles. A grand piano in the center of a room overlooking the city. Champagne in a bathtub. The same streets, a different planet.

The film lives in the space between these two worlds — hallways, service stairs, medical examiner corridors, cemetery corners — the Paris that neither world acknowledges.

---

## Key Locations

**Olivier's Loft**
Introduced as a temple to excess. By the third act, it's empty — stripped bare except for the grand piano, which ends up at Les Amandiers. The loft is a character: it opens the story and its emptiness closes a chapter of it.

**The Medical Examiner's Office**
Cold light. Steel tables. Pierre in a borrowed coat, pretending to be a medical student. One of the film's finest comedic set pieces — played entirely straight.

**Père-Lachaise Cemetery**
The funeral. The cremation. The pitbull. A place of mourning played for dark, uncomfortable comedy — and then, quietly, for genuine grief.

**The Road — Paris to Normandy (312 km)**
The film's emotional gear change. The road trip is where Pierre and Julia stop performing and start talking. Long silences. Wrong-turn arguments. The city dissolves behind them.

**Les Amandiers Foundation — Normandy Coast**
Warm. Bright. Modest. The emotional counterpoint to everything Paris has been. Madeleine's world — small, certain, tended — represents the only thing Olivier actually built that mattered. The foundation is where the film's heart lives.

**The Normandy Beach**
Night. Weed. Skinny-dipping. A jar of sand. The moment the characters are most themselves. The scene that makes the betrayal the next morning hurt more.

**The Infinity Club**
An after-hours nightclub near a rural train station, almost certainly not supposed to still be open. Under blacklight, the film's central mystery resolves. Darkness that reveals.

**The Film Set (Epilogue)**
A warehouse explosion. A pink wig. A camera pulling back. The final image is Pierre — finally living the version of his life he was chasing the whole time. The city is the same. He isn't.
