# FULL SYNOPSIS

## ACT ONE — THE DELIVERY GUY AND THE DEAD MAN

PIERRE SQUARE (late 20s) is an aspiring actor surviving as an Uber Eats cyclist in Paris. He fails auditions by day — including a recurring callback for the absurdist action role "Detective FOMO" — and pedals through rain and humiliation by night. He lives in a cramped studio apartment with two fish as his only companions. A man watching life pass him by.

One rainy night, Pierre delivers sushi to OLIVIER — a flamboyant, eccentric millionaire in his 40s, living in a cathedral-ceiling loft with a grand piano and a cat named Schrödinger. The delivery goes catastrophically wrong: sushi explodes across the apartment, soy sauce stains the piano, and Pierre earns a 1-star rating.

Later the same night, chased by muggers during a delivery, Pierre hides in Olivier's lobby — and stumbles into Olivier's birthday party. Olivier, high on cocaine and MDMA, mistakes Pierre for a banker named Jérôme. In the bathroom, in a reckless moment of euphoria, Olivier strips off his shirt to reveal his secret: his entire 24-word crypto seed phrase is tattooed across his body — scattered in words and a mysterious numbered dot pattern, including one word tattooed somewhere deeply inconvenient. He won millions in crypto. He trusts no one. So he became the wallet. They party all night.

The next morning, Pierre wakes on the couch. Olivier is dead in his bed — fatal overdose. A €50 bill sticks from his nostril. His neon sneaker still blinks.

Pierre photographs the tattoos from the body: 23 words and an indecipherable dot constellation. His phone dies mid-shoot. He unlocks Olivier's phone with the dead man's face. As he crouches over the body's lower half, the cat startles him. He falls face-first onto the corpse.

JULIA arrives — beautiful, guarded, mid-30s. She was at the party. She finds Pierre in his underwear, crouched over a dead body, holding a phone. She assumes the worst. Police arrive. Julia and Pierre hide in a closet, then flee via the service stairs. Julia grabs Olivier's laptop. In the chaos, she discovers the tattoo photos on Olivier's phone — and deletes them all, thinking Pierre is a pervert. She vanishes with the laptop.

## ACT TWO — THE TREASURE HUNT

Pierre chases the body. He follows the hearse through Paris by bicycle — pigeons, garbage bins, a spectacular crash. He tracks Olivier to the medical examiner's office, disguises himself as a second-year medical student, and infiltrates the autopsy. He secretly films the procedure, recovering the tattoos — until he's asked to perform a Y-incision and faints, cutting the footage short.

Back in his apartment, Pierre builds an investigation wall: 23 words, a blurry dot constellation, and "JULIA?" in red letters. He discovers the wallet holds €6,212,487.22. But a crypto seed phrase requires all 24 words in precise order. He has 23 words and an unsolved puzzle.

Julia returns. She needs Pierre's help to delete intimate content Olivier had of her from his encrypted laptop. Mutual suspicion. Uneasy alliance. 50/50 split. They attend Olivier's funeral — barely 10 people, a priest who clearly never met the man. Dark comedy ensues. Julia attempts to unlock the laptop with Olivier's dead finger in the funeral home. Pierre, tasked with retrieving the thrown laptop from a trash area, battles a pitbull and emerges wearing a funeral garland.

They use the finger to unlock the MacBook at a bar. The hard drive is empty — except for a hidden financial app showing the full portfolio. But they learn the body has been cremated. The tattoos are gone. The wallet remains locked.

Pierre enters all 23 words. **ACCESS DENIED.** The correct order is one of 24-factorial possible combinations — more than the age of the universe in seconds.

Julia has a lead: Olivier's tattoo artist. They visit a traditional Thai Sak Yant tattooist in Paris. Pierre endures bamboo-needle tattooing to reconstruct the dot constellation. When connected in order, the dots reveal **a giraffe** — a symbol of elevation, foresight, and love. The neck. Something is pointing toward something tall and hidden.

Julia reveals a crucial detail: Olivier had no memory. He must have written the correct order somewhere. They break into Olivier's now-vacated apartment during an open house viewing — Pierre in a pink wig, both posing as "Camila and Déborah Lafaille," a house-hunting couple. The loft is emptied. Only the grand piano remains. From the estate agent, they extract one name: Madeleine Lafarge. An adoptive mother. A care home. 312 kilometers away.

---

## ACT THREE — THE ROAD TRIP AND THE REVELATION

They drive to Les Amandiers Foundation, a countryside care home on the Normandy coast. Madeleine's caretaker ANA-MARIA warns them: Madeleine has dementia and doesn't know Olivier is dead. She thinks he's coming to visit on Sunday. She doesn't know he's been secretly paying for her care for years.

Madeleine mistakes Pierre for Olivier. It's devastating and tender. She serves them chocolate cake — Olivier's favorite. Pierre and Julia search his childhood bedroom: boxes of memorabilia, old photographs, a bong. Nothing.

That evening on the beach, they smoke Olivier's leftover weed. Skinny-dipping. A real conversation — what they'd do with the money, who they really are. Connection. Pierre collects sand in a small jar. Their shoulders touch.

The next morning, Julia sees a notification on Pierre's phone: **"Pierre, callback confirmed for Detective FOMO."** His name isn't Jérôme. He's not a banker. He's been lying the entire time. She's furious — not about the money. About the trust. They're strangers again.

Three hours until the Paris train. Pierre spots an after-hours club near the station. One last drink.

Under the club's blacklight, Pierre notices something on Julia's neck — a **fluorescent UV tattoo**, invisible in normal light. He leans in. It's a spiral of 24 words in precise order. Olivier tattooed the seed phrase's correct sequence on Julia herself, hidden as invisible ink beneath a decorative visible tattoo. The giraffe's meaning snaps into place: the long neck, pointing upward. Pointing to her. *"If he is chaos… you are order."*

They decode the tattoo together. They enter the wallet. **€6,212,487.22 unlocked.**

Pierre stares at the screen. He selects "Send." He selects 100%. Julia watches.

**The entire fortune is donated to Les Amandiers Foundation and Madeleine Lafarge** — the woman Olivier secretly loved as a mother, and the home sheltering people with nothing. The transaction confirms on Madeleine's iPad.

---

## EPILOGUE

Sand falls from a jar into water. The piano from Olivier's loft sits in the foundation's common room. A resident plays. Others smile. Madeleine watches, calm.

Then: a warehouse. Explosion. Smoke. A figure in a pink wig, gun drawn. It's Pierre — finally filming **Detective FOMO**. The camera pulls back. Film set. Julia watches from the crew, half-amused, half in love. The director calls cut. Pierre winks at Julia. Walks into rain. Explosion blows his wig sideways. Deadpan look into camera.

*"Detective FOMO — coming soon. It's going to blow your mind."*

**FADE OUT.**
