# THEMES

---

## The Worthlessness and Worth of Money

The entire plot is a chase for €6.2 million. The film ends with Pierre giving it all away. Olivier had millions and died alone on his birthday. The lottery billboard in the opening — a man who looks exactly like Pierre, tanned and smiling, cocktail in hand — frames the question the whole film is answering: *Is that what you want? Is that what it would take?*

The answer, when it comes, is quiet and irreversible.

---

## Identity and Deception

Pierre lies about his name from the moment he enters Olivier's party. Julia conceals her relationship to Olivier. Olivier hid his fortune in his own skin and scattered the key across someone else's body. Every major character is performing a version of themselves — and the film's emotional climax arrives when they stop.

The pink wig Pierre wears in disguise is the same one he wears at the film's end, playing Detective FOMO. The costume becomes the role he earned.

---

## Orphanhood and Belonging

Olivier chose his own birthday. He chose Madeleine. He chose to fund the care home anonymously, quietly, without recognition. He was an orphan who built his own family from scratch and never let anyone see it.

Pierre is emotionally adrift in a parallel way — floating through a city, invisible to it, waiting for a life that keeps not arriving. The film asks: what makes a home? What makes a family? And what do you owe the people who made you?

---

## The Body as Text

Olivier's body is a document. A wallet, a will, a love letter, and a puzzle. He transformed his own skin into a blockchain — a permanent, decentralized, tamper-proof record. The tattoos are read, photographed, reconstructed, and finally decoded, but only when the living body of someone he trusted is brought into the light.

The body as text also implies: you leave a record. Everything you do writes itself somewhere. Olivier's record was literally ink.

---

## Chance vs. Choice

A lottery billboard. A flat tire. A dead man on a birthday. Pierre's life appears governed entirely by bad luck — one-star ratings, pigeon collisions, auditions that almost happen. But every consequential moment in the film is a choice: he calls the ambulance. He photographs the tattoos. He follows the body. He donates the money.

Luck gets you to the room. Character decides what you do in it.

---

## Seeing What's Hidden

UV ink on a neck. A giraffe hidden in a constellation of dots. A seed phrase scattered across a human body. A fortune concealed in a dead man's skin. The film is obsessed with hidden information and the effort required to perceive what's been right in front of you the entire time.

Julia has carried the answer on her body for however long Olivier loved her. She didn't know. She couldn't see it. It took a blacklight and a man too broke to give up.
