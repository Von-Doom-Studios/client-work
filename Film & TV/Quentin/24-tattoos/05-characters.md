# CHARACTERS

---

## PIERRE SQUARE
**Late 20s | Aspiring Actor / Uber Eats Cyclist**

Lovable, chronically unlucky, and morally conflicted. Pierre survives on ramen and bad auditions, pedaling through Paris on a bike with a flat tire and ambitions that refuse to die. He's clumsy, self-deprecating, and wired with a mathematical mind he has no practical use for — until now.

Pierre is the kind of person who steals fish food and then can't accept a free bag out of guilt. He lies about his identity for most of the film, not out of malice, but out of a desperate need to be someone else. His journey is about becoming the person he already was — decent, brave, and worth something — without the six million dollars.

His last name, "Square" (as in πR²), is a running gag and a quiet metaphor: he keeps trying to explain it, no one cares, and it perfectly describes a man the world keeps trying to fit into the wrong shape.

**Arc:** From a man who watches life happen to him → to a man who chooses what his life means.

---

## JULIA
**Early–Mid 30s | Mysterious, Guarded, Precise**

Julia is sharp, elegant, and deeply private. She carries herself like someone who's learned the hard way that trust has a price. She entered Olivier's world through complicated circumstances and carries shame about intimate content he had of her — her original motivation in the story. 

She is pragmatic where Pierre is impulsive, strategic where he is chaotic, and emotionally contained where he is transparent. Her layers peel slowly: the guarded woman becomes a real person on the Normandy beach, and the real person is worth far more than what she's been protecting.

What she doesn't know — and what makes her the film's most elegant structural element — is that she literally carries the key to everything. Olivier trusted her with the most important thing he had: not money, but order.

**Arc:** From a woman protecting herself from exposure → to someone willing to be truly seen.

---

## OLIVIER
**40s | Eccentric Millionaire / Ghost**

Olivier dies on page 19. He haunts the rest of the film.

A former foster child who won a staggering crypto fortune, Olivier was reckless, generous, brilliant, and profoundly lonely. He threw lavish parties attended by hundreds of people who didn't know his last name. He had barely ten attendees at his funeral. He secretly funded his adoptive mother's care home. He named his cat Schrödinger.

His body is the film's central text — a treasure map, a love letter, a paranoid security system, and ultimately a final act of trust. The way he distributed the seed phrase across himself and Julia is the most intimate thing in the script: he was chaos, and he chose her as his order.

**Arc:** Present only in memory and consequence — yet the film's emotional center.

---

## MADELEINE LAFARGE
**80s | Olivier's Adoptive Mother**

Warm, sweet, and quietly devastating. Madeleine lives in the Les Amandiers Foundation, tended to by Ana-Maria, and doesn't know Olivier is dead. She thinks he's coming on Sunday.

When Pierre arrives and she mistakes him for her son, the film pivots. Whatever dark comedy got us here gives way to something genuine — an old woman who loved a difficult, lonely man, and a young man who suddenly understands what Olivier's millions were actually for.

She serves chocolate cake. She asks if Julia is "the one." She is the reason the fortune ends up where it does.

---

## SCHRÖDINGER
**Olivier's Cat**

Named for the quantum paradox of simultaneous states — alive and dead, present and absent. Schrödinger appears at key moments throughout the film, disrupting evidence, providing comic relief, and functioning as an almost spectral link to Olivier. The cat's name is the film's most elegant piece of foreshadowing: Olivier himself exists, for the entire runtime, in exactly that state.

---

## ANA-MARIA
**60s | Caretaker, Les Amandiers Foundation**

Protective, suspicious, and fiercely devoted to Madeleine. She serves as gatekeeper and exposition — the first person to articulate, plainly, who Olivier really was beneath the loft and the champagne.
