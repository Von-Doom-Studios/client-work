## Voice rules

Builder-confident: declarative, technical when it earns its keep, dry when humor lands, plain when humor doesn't. The reader is a developer or operator who recognizes real engineering work and tunes out marketing performance.

What this means in practice:

- **Lead with the problem or the proof, not with a punchline.** On product and proof posts, wit follows the proof. Wit never leads. (Comedy posts are the exception by design - see comedy rules below.)
- **Specificity over abstraction.** Real flows, real receipts, real numbers, real failure modes. Never "transforming," "revolutionizing," "reimagining."
- **One idea per post.** If a post needs two ideas, it's two posts.
- **Sentence case on X is the default.** All-lowercase as a permanent affectation is not.
- **No em dashes.** Hyphens or restructure.
- **Banned phrases:** "excited to announce," "thrilled," "we're so proud," "gm fam," "revolutionizing," "game-changing," "the future of," "stay tuned," "you guessed it," "your wallet wallet" and similar repetitive-word stunts.
- **No empty hooks.** No "let me tell you a story," no "here's what nobody is talking about."
- **X length:** target 240 characters for a punchy single, longer when a receipt or proof needs the space. Threads only when one tweet truly won't carry the load.

## Comedy rules (Wednesday slot)

Comedy posts run every Wednesday. They are the "we're not just selling" lever. Rules:

- **Punches up at incumbents (banks, card networks, broken checkouts, IATA, KYC theater) or sideways at industry absurdity.** Never punches down.
- **In-thesis.** A comedy post about an unrelated meme is not a Brij post. The joke has to be a Brij-shaped joke.
- **On-voice.** Dry, observational, occasionally absurd. Setup-payoff structure. Not "lol agents go brrr."
- **The brand is still recognizable.** A reader who has never seen Brij before should be able to infer what the product is from the post.
- **No hashtags. No emojis as punchlines. No reaction GIFs as the post.**
