Research-based tactics for each platform. Built for algorithmic distribution and growth.

## X/TWITTER ENGINE

**Goal:** 2,000-5,000 followers. Primary platform for crypto/AI audience.

### Algorithm Mechanics (Based on 2025/2026 Research)
- **Engagement rate in first hour** determines distribution
- **Replies > likes** for algorithm boosting  
- **Quote tweets with commentary** spread faster than retweets
- **Threads get higher reach** than single tweets
- **Video content** gets 3x more impressions

### Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: "The Experiment" (40% of content)**
Live testing and results. Algorithm loves real data.

Examples:
- Price comparisons: Same flight, 3 sites, screenshot results
- Speed tests: "From 'I need a flight' to confirmation code: 30 seconds"
- Cost breakdowns: "What a BRIJ booking actually costs: Search $0.10, Reserve $0.10, Book [price]. That's it."

**Pillar 2: "Behind the Build" (30% of content)**  
Technical threads that developers share.

Examples:
- "How x402 works under the hood" (code snippets + architecture)
- "Why we chose Solana for escrow" (technical reasoning)
- "The API that airlines don't want you to have" (access control breakdown)

**Pillar 3: "David vs Goliath" (20% of content)**
Small team vs big incumbents. Resonates with startup community.

Examples:
- "Airlines spent $4.2B on pricing manipulation. We spent $0.20 on an honest API call."
- "Google Flights: 40 engineers. BRIJ Travel: 3 people. Guess which one doesn't track you?"

**Pillar 4: "User Stories" (10% of content)**
Social proof from real users.

### Posting Strategy

**Daily Schedule:**
- **9 AM EST:** Main thread or announcement
- **2 PM EST:** Reply/engage with ecosystem 
- **7 PM EST:** Standalone insight or user story

**Weekly Rhythm:**
- **Monday:** Major launch/announcement thread
- **Tuesday:** Technical deep dive
- **Wednesday:** Experiment results
- **Thursday:** Industry commentary/reaction
- **Friday:** Week recap + weekend hook
- **Weekend:** Community engagement only

### Growth Tactics

**1. Technical Thread Sharing**
- Tag relevant developers: @solana, @phantom, @jupyter
- Cross-post to Hacker News when technical content performs well
- Share in Discord/Telegram channels where builders hang out

**2. Quote Tweet Strategy**  
- QT airline/travel pain points with BRIJ solution
- QT AI/agent announcements with "This is why agents need infrastructure"
- QT user feedback with appreciation

**3. Reply Chain Hijacking**
- Monitor conversations about flight booking frustration
- Reply with helpful context (not direct pitches)
- Build reputation as helpful technical expert

## EMAIL ENGINE

**Goal:** 10,000 subscribers with >30% open rates and behavioral automation.

### List Building System

**Lead Magnets:**
1. **"The x402 Developer Guide"** (PDF) — Technical audience
2. **"Agent Economics Report"** (PDF) — Investment/business audience  
3. **"Flight Booking Price Study"** (PDF) — Consumer audience

**Capture Points:**
- Homepage above fold: "Get the x402 Developer Guide"
- Blog posts: Content upgrades specific to each post
- Post-booking: "Want updates on wallet launch?"
- Social media bios: Links to lead magnets, not generic signup

### Email Sequences (Behavioral Triggers)

**Sequence 1: Welcome Series (3 emails, 5 days)**
- Day 0: Guide delivery + what BRIJ does in 2 sentences
- Day 3: "How we're different from every other travel tool"
- Day 5: "Ready to try? Here's how" (direct CTA to book)

**Sequence 2: Post-Booking Automation**
- Hour 1: Booking confirmation + referral offer
- Day 3: "How was your experience?" (feedback collection)
- Day 14: "Going somewhere else?" (re-engagement)

**Sequence 3: Abandoned Search (Users who search but don't book)**
- Hour 2: "Price changed? Here's what happened"
- Day 1: Social proof ("487 people booked flights yesterday")
- Day 3: Limited-time offer or bonus

**Sequence 4: Feature Launch Announcements**
- Wallet launch: "Your agent just got a bank account"
- New integrations: "Now works with [Claude/ChatGPT/etc]"
- Credit card onboarding: "No crypto needed anymore"

### Content Calendar (Weekly)

**Tuesday:** Feature updates or product news
**Friday:** Weekly roundup: "This week in agent infrastructure"

**No more than 2 emails per week.** Quality over volume.

## REDDIT ENGINE

**Strategy:** Helpful participation, not promotion. Build reputation first, link second.

### Target Subreddits
- **r/solana** (125k members) - Technical posts about x402, escrow
- **r/ChatGPT** (180k) - Agent capability demonstrations  
- **r/travel** (4.2M) - Consumer angle, price comparison studies
- **r/MachineLearning** (2.8M) - Technical architecture posts

### Content Approach

**r/solana Posts:**
- "Built the first x402 merchant on Solana - AMA"
- "Escrow-backed flight bookings - here's the architecture"
- "Why we chose Solana for agent infrastructure"

**r/ChatGPT Posts:**
- "I connected my AI agent to live flight inventory"
- "Teaching ChatGPT to book real flights (via MCP)"
- "The future of AI agents: from reasoning to transacting"

**r/travel Posts:**
- "I tracked airline price manipulation across booking sites"
- "Your searches are being used against you - here's proof"
- "Booked 100 flights with AI vs manually - cost comparison"

### Engagement Rules
- **Never lead with links** - value first, link in comments if asked
- **Answer questions** in target subreddits before posting own content
- **Use real data** - screenshots, receipts, actual examples
- **Be honest about limitations** - Reddit rewards authenticity

## CONTENT TESTING FRAMEWORK

### Weekly A/B Tests
- **X:** Thread hooks (question vs statement vs statistic)
- **Email:** Subject lines (curiosity vs value vs urgency)  
- **YouTube:** Thumbnails and titles
- **Reddit:** Post titles and opening paragraphs

### Success Metrics Per Platform
- **X:** Comments > likes ratio, profile clicks, follower growth
- **Email:** Open rate >30%, click rate >5%, unsubscribe <2%
- **YouTube:** Watch time >50%, comment engagement, subscriber conversion
- **Reddit:** Comment volume, upvote ratio, referral traffic quality

### Optimization Cycle
**Monday:** Review weekend performance data
**Tuesday:** Implement changes based on data
**Wednesday:** Test new formats/approaches
**Thursday:** Double down on what worked
**Friday:** Plan next week's tests

**The goal is systematic improvement, not viral luck.**
