# Brand Voice — BRIJ

---

## The Core Voice

BRIJ speaks like a builder talking to builders. Confident without arrogance. Technical without being exclusionary. Direct without being cold.

The manifesto sets the tone: "Per call. Per millisecond. Per cent." / "One call. Best price. Done." / "Agents don't just need intelligence. They need infrastructure."

That cadence — punchy, rhythmic, specific — is the voice.

**Precise.** Say exactly what the thing does. Not more, not less.
**Confident.** We built something that didn't exist. Say so without hedging.
**Grounded.** The vision is massive. The communication stays rooted in what's shipped.
**Human.** Infrastructure companies default to robotic copy. BRIJ doesn't.

---

## Universal Rules

1. **Never say "revolutionize," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," or "next-generation."** These words signal you have nothing specific to say.

2. **Lead with what it does, not what it is.**
   - ❌ "BRIJ is an agent-native infrastructure platform for Solana."
   - ✅ "Your agent can book a flight, pay for an API call, and settle on-chain. That's BRIJ."

3. **Never explain crypto to people who know crypto.** Phase 1 audience knows what USDC is.

4. **Never assume crypto knowledge from people who don't.** Phase 2/3 audience doesn't care about Solana.

5. **Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space.** The copy should feel light.

6. **No exclamation marks.** Excitement comes from substance, not punctuation.

7. **Code is copy.** For developers, a clean code snippet communicates more than a paragraph.

8. **Show the delta.** Before → After. Traditional → BRIJ.

## Voice by Audience

### For Geeks (Phase 1)
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. A builder showing you what they built.
**Vocabulary:** x402, escrow PDA, MCP, pay-per-call, USDC, settlement, OpenAPI, curl.
**Format:** Threads with code. Short demos. GitHub READMEs.

Examples:
> "Four HTTP calls. Search, reserve, fund, book. Your agent just booked a flight on real airline inventory. x402 settled, escrow-backed on Solana."

> "We spent 6 months asking: what if paying for an API was just an HTTP status code? That's x402. 402 Payment Required → pay → 200 OK."

> "The OpenRouter for Solana. Read state, write transactions, pay for services, onramp fiat. One integration."

### For Early Adopters (Phase 2)
**Tone:** Knowledgeable guide. Showing you something cool you'll immediately want.
**Vocabulary:** AI agent, book a flight, fair price, no tracking, automatic, on your behalf.
**Format:** Short-form video, comparison graphics, "I tried this" threads.

Examples:
> "I asked my AI to book a flight to Barcelona. 30 seconds later, confirmed. No tabs. No price games."

> "Airlines track your searches and raise prices the more you look. Your AI agent doesn't have cookies or browser history. It just gets the price."

### For Mainstream (Phase 3)
**Tone:** Simple, trustworthy, warm.
**Vocabulary:** Book, flight, save, easy, safe, confirmed.
**Format:** Testimonials, price comparisons, social proof.

Examples:
> "Book flights with AI. Pay with your card. Get confirmed in seconds."

## Voice by Channel

### X/Twitter
- Maximum density. Every word earns its place.
- Threads for launches. Single tweets for daily presence.
- Never start a tweet with "Excited to announce" or "We're thrilled."
- Quote-tweet users who try the product — highest-leverage action.

### YouTube
- Show the result in the first 5 seconds.
- Conversational narration, not scripted-corporate.
- Real demos, real bookings.
- Under 60 seconds for launch clips. Under 5 minutes for deep dives.

### Reddit
- Post as a builder sharing work, not a brand announcing.
- Answer technical questions in detail.
- Be honest about limitations. Reddit punishes corporate defensiveness.

### Email
- Subject lines: specific, not clever. "BRIJ Travel is live — book flights with your AI agent."
- One message, one CTA. No filler newsletters.
- Only send when there's something worth saying.

### GitHub
- READMEs are marketing. Treat them like landing pages.
- Working code examples. No broken links.
- Respond to issues promptly and honestly.

## What BRIJ Never Sounds Like

❌ "We're building the future of AI-powered commerce."
❌ "Join the revolution."
❌ "Seamless, frictionless, end-to-end solution."
❌ "BRIJ leverages blockchain technology to enable..."
❌ Any sentence that works if you swap the company name.

## What BRIJ Always Sounds Like

✅ Specific — you know what the product does after one sentence.
✅ Confident — we built this, it works, here's proof.
✅ Honest — if something isn't ready, we say so.
✅ Builder-first — written by people who build, for people who build.
