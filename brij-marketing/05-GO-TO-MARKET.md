# Go-to-Market — BRIJ

Phased launch strategy. Each phase has a defined audience, core message, deliverables, and success criteria. Do not skip phases. Do not market Phase 3 messaging while in Phase 1.

---

## Phase 1: Geek Launch (Weeks 1–2) — ACTIVE

### Objective
First 100 real users on the travel API. Establish BRIJ Travel as the world's first agentic flight booking. Generate organic word-of-mouth from builders.

### Audience
Developers, crypto-native builders, AI/agent enthusiasts. See [03-AUDIENCES.md](./03-AUDIENCES.md) — Audience 1.

### Core Message
**"Your AI agent can book flights. Right now. For real."**

### Week 1: Launch

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|-----|--------|---------|-------|
| Mon | Shortened technical demo video (15–30 sec) | X | Anthony |
| Mon | Launch tweet thread (5–7 tweets: what it is, how it works, code snippet, link) | X | Anthony |
| Mon | Post in AI/crypto communities (r/solana, r/ChatGPT, r/LocalLLaMA, Hacker News) | Reddit/HN | All |
| Tue | Share to personal networks, tag relevant builders | X | All 3 |
| Wed | "How it works" technical thread (x402 flow, escrow, 4 API calls) | X | Anthony |
| Thu | DM outreach to 10–15 small AI/crypto builders with demo link | X DMs | David |
| Fri | Share to Eman + close network | Direct | David |

### Week 2: Momentum

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|-----|--------|---------|-------|
| Mon | "Behind the build" thread — why x402, why travel, the OpenRouter analogy | X | Anthony/Antoine |
| Tue | Amplify user content — repost anyone who tried it | X | All |
| Wed | Technical deep dive: how escrow works on Solana | X / Blog | Antoine |
| Thu | "What's next" tease — wallet integration, platform vision | X | Anthony |
| Fri | Review Week 1–2 results. Adjust Phase 2 plan. | Internal | All |

### Email List Seeding (Phase 1)
- Add email capture to brij.fi (above the fold, "Get early access" or "Join 1,000+ developers")
- Capture emails from premium access signups
- Target: 500 emails by end of Phase 1

### Capacity Management
- $5,000 buffer for standard bookings
- $5,000 buffer for premium access
- When buffer exhausted: "High demand — request premium access" (not a waitlist)
- Premium fee: $5–10 to qualify real purchase intent

### Ad Spend
$200–500 on X promoted tweets targeting AI/crypto/developer interests.

### Success Criteria
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Unique API users | 100+ |
| Completed bookings | 15+ |
| Organic mentions on X | 10+ |
| Email signups | 500 |
| GitHub stars/forks | 50+ |

## Phase 2: Wallet Launch (Weeks 3–6)

### Objective
Launch the smart wallet. Expand audience from API geeks to tech-comfortable consumers. Accelerate email list and social growth.

### Audience
Early adopters. See [03-AUDIENCES.md](./03-AUDIENCES.md) — Audience 2.

### Core Message
**"Tell your AI where you want to go. It handles the rest."**

### Deliverables

| Item | Format | Channel |
|------|--------|---------|
| Wallet launch video (30–60 sec, consumer-facing) | Short-form video | X, YouTube, TikTok |
| "I booked a flight with AI" experience thread | Thread/carousel | X |
| Price comparison content (traditional vs. agent booking) | Graphics + thread | X, YouTube |
| "Airlines track your searches" awareness content | Short-form video | TikTok, X |
| YouTube explainer: "How to book flights with your AI agent" | Video (2–3 min) | YouTube |
| Landing page update: wallet CTA on travel.brij.fi | Web | — |
| Email blast to Phase 1 signups | Email | — |

### Email Growth (Phase 2)
- Weekly email sends begin (1/week max)
- Content: what's new, user stories, travel tips with agent angle
- Lead magnet: "AI Travel Guide — How agents are changing flight booking"
- Target: 2,500 total emails by end of Phase 2

### Social Growth (Phase 2)
- Daily posting on X (1–2x/day)
- YouTube Shorts (2–3/week)
- TikTok begins (2–3/week)
- Target: 1,000 followers on X by end of Phase 2

### Ad Spend
$500–1,000 on X and YouTube. Test 2–3 creatives, kill underperformers by day 3.

### Success Criteria
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Wallet signups | 300+ |
| Wallet-funded bookings | 75+ |
| YouTube explainer views | 5,000+ |
| Email list | 2,500 |
| X followers | 1,000 |
| Email open rate | >30% |
| Repeat bookings | 20+ |

## Phase 3: Mainstream (Weeks 7+)

### Objective
Credit card onboarding. Eliminate crypto friction. Scale to mainstream audience. Hit milestone targets.

### Audience
Everyone. See [03-AUDIENCES.md](./03-AUDIENCES.md) — Audience 3.

### Core Message
**"Book flights with AI. Pay with your card."**

### Deliverables

| Item | Format | Channel |
|------|--------|---------|
| Mainstream launch video (15–30 sec, zero jargon) | Short-form video | All social |
| Testimonial/case study content from Phase 1–2 users | Video/thread | All social |
| "How much did you save?" comparison content | Web tool / graphics | brij.fi, social |
| PR outreach to tech press (TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired) | Press | Direct |
| Google Ads: "book flights with AI" | Paid search | Google |
| Meta/Instagram ads targeting travel audiences | Paid social | Meta |
| Email sequences: welcome series, booking follow-ups, re-engagement | Email | — |

### Email Growth (Phase 3)
- Automated welcome sequence (3 emails over 7 days)
- Post-booking follow-up email
- Monthly newsletter with travel savings highlights
- Target: **10,000 total email signups**

### Social Growth (Phase 3)
- Scale what worked in Phase 2
- Influencer partnerships (travel + tech creators)
- User-generated content campaigns
- Target: **2,000–5,000 followers across platforms**

### Ad Spend
Scale based on Phase 2 CAC data. Target: CPA < booking margin.

### Success Criteria
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total bookings | 1,000+ |
| Credit card conversion | >10% of visitors |
| Email list | **10,000** |
| Social followers | **2,000–5,000** |
| Press coverage | 1+ major outlet |
| Organic > paid growth | Organic majority |

## The Weekly Calendar Rule

Anthony owns the weekly marketing calendar. Every Thursday/Friday, the next week's plan is drafted:
1. What are we posting?
2. Where are we posting it?
3. Who is responsible?
4. What's the narrative thread for the week?

Reviewed by the team. Then executed. No exceptions.

The calendar is the cure for inconsistency.
