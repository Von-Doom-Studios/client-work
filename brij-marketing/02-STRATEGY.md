## Positioning (one sentence)

Brij is the payments layer for AI agents on Solana - smart wallet, on-ramps, and pay-per-call service access over x402.

The wedge proof is travel.brij.fi: an agent can find, book, and pay for real flights using x402. The product is the proof. The company is the rails underneath.

## Audience (ranked)

Every post serves at least one. If a post doesn't, it doesn't get made.

1. **Agent / AI infrastructure builders.** Developers shipping with LangChain, CrewAI, Mastra, Vercel AI SDK, Letta, Eliza. They hit payment walls. We solve that. Primary buyer of Platform APIs.
2. **x402 / Solana devrel and ecosystem.** Coinbase devrel, Solana Foundation, Helius, Jupiter, Phantom infrastructure. Amplifiers, not customers. Earned reposts here are worth 10x algorithmic reach.
3. **Crypto-native builders looking for "it actually works" stories.** Bankless, The Rollup, Lightspeed audiences. They share. They drive earned media.
4. **Curious operators and founders outside crypto.** People who hear "agent books a flight with crypto" and want to see it. Lower priority, but they convert to email well because the demo is the hook.

We are not posting for retail crypto, memecoin traders, or general AI consumers.

## Channel

X only.

- **Handle:** @brij_fi
- **Posting cadence:** 3 posts per week. Mon / Wed / Fri. One of the three is comedy.
- **Engagement:** daily, driven by the queue produced by the external X-watcher agent reading [07-ENGAGEMENT.md](./07-ENGAGEMENT.md). 15-20 minutes/day floor.

Everything else (LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, IG, Farcaster) is explicitly out of scope. If anything else is added later, it requires a strategy update here first.

## Comedy slot (1 of 3 X posts per week)

One of the three weekly posts is humor-led. The intent is not "selling" three times a week. The intent is to show the brand has a personality, generate organic shares from people who don't normally repost B2B fintech, and recruit followers who aren't already in the buyer pool.

Comedy rules:

- Punches up at the incumbent (banks, card networks, broken checkouts, IATA, KYC theater) or punches sideways at the absurdity of how things work today. Never punches down.
- Stays in-thesis. A comedy post about an unrelated meme is not a Brij post.
- Stays on-voice. Dry, observational, occasionally absurd. Not "lol agents go brrr." Not lowercase-Twitter shitposting as a substitute for a joke.
- The brand should still be recognizable in the post even when the joke is the point.

If a comedy post can't be told without dragging an unrelated topic in, it doesn't ship that week.

## Targets

**90-day checkpoint (2026-08-21):**

- 500 X followers (net new from baseline at handoff)
- 1,000 email signups
- 50 completed bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 1 piece of earned media in a crypto outlet OR 1 Coinbase devrel co-marketing moment
- 3 developer teams in conversation about Platform integration

If by day 90 we're under 250 emails or under 10 bookings, the strategy gets re-cut. We don't grind harder on a broken hook.

**12-month target (2027-05-22):**

- 5,000 X followers
- 10,000 email signups
- 1,000 bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 10+ external products live on Platform APIs

## Growth math

The numbers aren't hopes. They're a function of cadence, conversion, and amplification.

**Followers - getting to 5,000 in 12 months:**

- Net new needed: ~5,000 in 52 weeks = ~100 net new followers per week average.
- Drivers: 3 posts/week + 15-20 minutes/day of engagement on target accounts via [07-ENGAGEMENT.md](./07-ENGAGEMENT.md) + 2-3 amplifications per quarter (earned media, ecosystem quote-posts, campaign moments).
- Without amplification events, organic baseline lands at ~30-50 net new followers/week. The math only works if we land at least 1 amplification per month (ecosystem repost, earned media mention, a thread that breaks 100k impressions).
- Comedy posts are an amplification lever. They reach outside the buyer pool. We expect 1 in 4 to noticeably outperform baseline engagement.
- Paid amplification: see [03-BUDGET.md](./03-BUDGET.md). Boost the top-performing organic post each month.

**Emails - getting to 10,000 in 12 months:**

- Net new needed: ~10,000 in 52 weeks = ~190 emails per week average.
- Email arrives via: (a) site capture on brij.fi and travel.brij.fi, (b) post-booking capture on travel.brij.fi, (c) newsletter referrals, (d) campaign-specific landing pages, (e) partner co-marketing email swaps.
- Conversion assumptions: 2-3% visitor-to-email on the homepage hero CTA, 5-8% post-booking capture, 10-15% partner co-marketing list-share rate.
- Site traffic required to hit 190/week organically: ~7,000-9,000 weekly site visits. travel.brij.fi can drive a chunk of this if a demo post pops on X.
- The math fails without (a) capture forms going live in week 1 (briefed in [08-NEWSLETTER.md](./08-NEWSLETTER.md)) and (b) at least 1 partner co-marketing moment per quarter.

If month-2 numbers project below trajectory, escalate: more paid spend ([03-BUDGET.md](./03-BUDGET.md)), more aggressive partner outreach (briefed to Ops), or re-cut the strategy.

## What we are (and stay)

- A rails company. Travel is the wedge, not the category.
- A builder-facing brand. Voice lives in dev-tooling territory.
- A "ship and show" brand. Receipts beat takes.
- The first or earliest mover in agent-payments on Solana via x402.

## What we are not (and never drift into)

- Not a travel app. We don't compete with Expedia.
- Not a memecoin or token play. No token narrative in marketing.
- Not a wallet for humans. Espresso Cash was; Brij isn't.
- Not "AI for crypto." We're crypto rails for AI.
- Not a thought leadership shop. No 8-tweet threads about "the future of agents."

If a piece of content reads like it could belong to one of those, it doesn't ship.
