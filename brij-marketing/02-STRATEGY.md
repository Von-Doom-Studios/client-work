## Positioning

Brij is the payments layer for AI agents on Solana - smart wallet, on-ramps, and pay-per-call service access.

The wedge proof is travel.brij.fi: an AI agent can find, book, and pay for real flights. The product is the proof. The company is the rails underneath.

## Audience (ranked)

Every post serves at least one. If a post doesn't, it doesn't get made.

1. **Agent / AI infrastructure builders.** Devs on LangChain, CrewAI, Mastra, Vercel AI SDK, Letta, Eliza. They hit payment walls; we solve that. Primary buyer.
2. **Solana / x402 ecosystem.** Coinbase devrel, Solana Foundation, Helius, Jupiter, Phantom. Amplifiers. One repost here is worth 50 generic likes.
3. **Crypto-native builders who share "it actually works" stories.** Bankless, The Rollup, Lightspeed audiences.
4. **Curious operators and founders outside crypto.** People who hear "an AI booked a flight with crypto" and want to see it.

Not for: retail crypto, memecoin traders, general AI consumers.

## Voice

Builder-confident: declarative, plain, dry. The reader is a developer or operator. They tune out marketing performance.

- **Plain language.** No PNR, IATA, escrow PDA, or other insider jargon in the lead line. If a term needs to appear, it earns its place by being the joke or the punchline.
- **Lead with the picture, not the spec.** Show the thing happening before naming the protocol.
- **One idea per post.** If you need two, write two posts.
- **No em dashes. No hashtags. No emojis as punchlines.**
- **Banned phrases:** "excited to announce," "thrilled," "we're so proud," "gm fam," "revolutionizing," "game-changing," "the future of," "stay tuned."
- **Length:** target 240 characters. Longer when a receipt needs space. Threads only when one tweet truly won't carry it.

## Voice tests (apply before any copy ships)

Mechanics for hooks, length, formatting, and replies live in [09-POSTING-PLAYBOOK.md](./09-POSTING-PLAYBOOK.md). These two voice tests run separately, every time:

- **Read it out loud.** Does it sound like a person? If it sounds like a press release, rewrite.
- **Agency bar.** Would Phantom, Linear, or Coinbase's social team put their name on this? If no, rewrite.
- **Brand confidence check.** Does the copy paint Brij or Solana in a confident light, with no hedges like "closer to working than people realize"? If no, rewrite.

## Comedy (Wednesday)

One funny post per week is the "we're not just selling" lever. Comedy posts have to be actually funny, not just contrast posts dressed as jokes.

Rules:

- **Real joke structure.** Setup, misdirection, payoff. Or specificity that lands the surprise. Not "X does this, we do that."
- **Punches up at incumbents (banks, card networks, broken checkouts) or sideways at industry absurdity.** Never down.
- **In-thesis.** A meme that isn't Brij-shaped is not a Brij post.
- **Brand still recognizable.** A new reader can infer the product from the joke.
- **Read it out loud before shipping.** If you don't laugh, smile, or at least exhale through your nose, it's not ready.

## Targets

**90-day (2026-08-21):**
- 500 net new X followers
- 1,000 email signups
- 50 bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 1 earned-media moment OR 1 ecosystem co-marketing
- 3 dev teams in conversation about Platform integration

If we're under 250 emails or 10 bookings by day 90, the strategy gets re-cut.

**12-month (2027-05-22):**
- 5,000 X followers
- 10,000 email signups
- 1,000 bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 10+ external products live on Platform APIs

## Growth math (short version)

- Followers: 5,000 in 52 weeks = ~100 net/week. 3 posts + daily engagement + 1 amplification per month gets there.
- Emails: 10,000 in 52 weeks = ~190 emails/week. Site capture forms + 1 partner co-marketing moment per quarter or the math fails.

Numbers re-evaluated monthly. Paid escalation rules in [03-BUDGET.md](./03-BUDGET.md).
