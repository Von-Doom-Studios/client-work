# 01 — THESIS

## Positioning (one sentence)

**BRIJ is the payments layer for AI agents — and we're proving it works by letting an agent book your next flight.**

## The bet

The next billion wallet users won't be humans. They'll be AI agents acting on behalf of humans. The infrastructure to let agents *transact* — not just chat — is being built right now, and most of crypto is still arguing about it on Twitter.

We're not arguing. We shipped travel.brij.fi. An AI agent that finds, books, and pays for real flights using x402. It works today.

That product is not the company. It's the proof. The company is the rails underneath it — Read / Write / Pay / Onramp — that any builder can use to give their agent a wallet and the ability to spend.

## Why now

Three things are true simultaneously, and that's the window:

1. **x402 just shipped.** Coinbase released the protocol for agent payments. It's months old. The ecosystem of products built on it is small enough to count. BRIJ is one of them. Early-mover narrative is available — but only for the next 6–9 months before it's saturated.
2. **Agent frameworks are hitting payment walls.** LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGPT-style builders can plan and execute — but the moment an agent needs to *pay for something*, the demo dies. We solve that. They have the demand; we have the infra.
3. **Consumer crypto is starved for "it actually works" stories.** The market has spent two years on memecoins and vaporware. A working product where you tell an AI "book me a flight to Lisbon" and it does — pays, confirms, sends the ticket — is a thumbstop. It cuts through.

If we wait six months, x402 has a dozen serious products on it and we're competing on features. Today we can own the narrative.

## What we are NOT

This matters more than what we are. If we drift into any of these, we lose.

- **Not a travel app.** travel.brij.fi is a wedge, not a category. We don't compete with Expedia. We compete with Stripe-for-agents.
- **Not a memecoin / token play.** No token launch on the roadmap drives this marketing. If it changes, the strategy changes. Until then, zero token energy in our content.
- **Not a wallet for humans.** Espresso Cash was. BRIJ isn't. Every time we sound like a consumer wallet, we confuse the buyer (developers).
- **Not "AI for crypto."** That phrase is dead. We're crypto rails for AI. The order matters.
- **Not a thought leadership shop.** We don't write 8-tweet threads about "the future of agents." We ship things and show them working. Builders trust receipts, not takes.

## The contrarian belief

Most teams in this space believe **agents need permission, custody, and abstraction to handle money safely**, so they're building elaborate multi-sig / approval-gated systems that take 18 months to ship.

We believe **agents need money the way websites needed HTTPS — a thin, opinionated layer that just works.** Ship the 80% version now. Real users will tell us what the dangerous edges are. Six months of perfect security architecture on a product nobody uses is worse than three months of imperfect product getting hit by real traffic.

This belief is why we shipped Travel before the Wallet. Most teams would do the opposite.

## Success metrics

**90 days (Aug 21):**
- 1,000 email signups on BRIJ
- 500 X followers on @brij (or whichever handle we lock in)
- 50 completed bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 3 developer teams in conversation about Platform integration
- 1 piece of earned media in a crypto outlet (The Block, Decrypt, Bankless, or equivalent) OR 1 Coinbase devrel co-marketing moment

**12 months (May 22, 2027):**
- 10,000 email signups
- 2,000–5,000 social followers (X is primary; LinkedIn secondary)
- 1,000 bookings on travel.brij.fi
- 10+ external products live on Platform APIs
- Platform revenue measurable (not zero)

If by day 90 we have <250 emails and <10 bookings, the thesis is wrong and we re-cut the strategy. We don't grind harder on a broken hook.
