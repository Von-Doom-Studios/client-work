# Audiences — BRIJ

BRIJ has three consumer audiences that arrive in sequence, plus an ongoing B2B audience. The phased rollout determines which audience is active. Marketing for the wrong audience at the wrong time wastes budget and confuses positioning.

---

## Audience 1: The Geeks (Phase 1 — Active Now)

### Who They Are
Developers, crypto-native builders, AI/agent enthusiasts, Solana ecosystem participants. They run MCP servers, build with LangChain, use Grok/ChatGPT/Claude as daily tools, hold USDC, and are comfortable with APIs and terminal commands.

### Demographics
- Age: 22–40
- Skew: Male, globally distributed, concentrated in US/EU/SEA tech hubs
- Platforms: X/Twitter, GitHub, Reddit (r/solana, r/LocalLLaMA, r/ChatGPT, r/singularity), Discord, Hacker News

### What They Care About
- **Does it actually work?** They will try the API before reading the landing page.
- **Is it technically sound?** They'll read the x402 spec and the OpenAPI schema.
- **Is it novel?** "First agentic flight booking" is interesting. "AI-powered booking" is not.
- **Can they build on it?** The platform matters more than travel to this audience.

### What Turns Them Off
- Marketing speak ("revolutionize your experience")
- Vague claims without technical backing
- Products that require email signup before showing what they do
- Overly polished videos that feel corporate

### Channels
X/Twitter, GitHub, Reddit, dev Discord servers, Hacker News, AI/crypto YouTube

### Milestone Role
These users become the social proof for Phase 2. Their tweets, their GitHub stars, their Reddit posts validate BRIJ for the next audience.

## Audience 2: Early Adopters (Phase 2 — Wallet Launch)

### Who They Are
Tech-comfortable consumers who use AI tools daily but are not developers. They have ChatGPT or Grok accounts. May or may not hold crypto. Open to new tools but need friction removed.

### Demographics
- Age: 25–45
- Broader gender distribution than Phase 1
- Urban, educated, digitally fluent
- Platforms: X/Twitter, YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, mainstream tech podcasts

### What They Care About
- **Time and money saved.** "My agent booked my flight in 30 seconds" is compelling.
- **Safety.** Escrow-backed bookings, refund capability, spending limits.
- **Ease.** If they need to understand Solana to use it, they're gone.
- **Fairness.** "Airlines can't track your agent" is a powerful message.

### What Turns Them Off
- Crypto jargon ("USDC on Solana" means nothing to them)
- Terminal commands in demo videos
- Feeling like a beta tester

### Channels
YouTube (tutorials + comparisons), TikTok/Reels (short demos), X/Twitter (consumer threads), Email (launch announcements), Paid ads (X, YouTube)

### Milestone Role
These users drive the email list and social follower growth. They're the engine for hitting 10K signups and 2–5K followers.

---

## Audience 3: Mainstream (Phase 3 — Credit Card Onboarding)

### Who They Are
Everyone who books flights. Don't know what Solana is. Don't care. Want the flight booked, at a fair price, with minimal effort.

### Demographics
- Age: 18–60
- Everyone
- Platforms: Whatever they already use

### What They Care About
- **Price.** Is it cheaper or the same?
- **Trust.** Is this a real company? Will I get my ticket?
- **Ease.** Can I pay with my credit card?
- **Proof.** What are other people saying?

### Channels
Google Search (ads + SEO), Meta/Instagram ads, YouTube, PR/press coverage, referrals, email marketing

### Milestone Role
This is where scale happens. 10K email signups, mass social following, press coverage.

---

## Audience 4: API Providers & Wallet Developers (Ongoing)

### Who They Are
Companies and developers with APIs that agents might use, or wallet teams looking for fiat rails.

From the manifesto: "Whether you're a wallet looking for fiat rails, a developer exposing APIs to agents, or a team building autonomous agents on Solana — we're the layer underneath."

### What They Care About
- Revenue without operational overhead (list once, earn per call)
- Easy integration
- Transaction volume proof

### Channels
Developer docs, x402 spec, GitHub, Solana ecosystem events, direct outreach, technical blog posts

---

## The Audience Sequence Rule

You market to the active phase audience. Period.

A technical tweet about x402 escrow does not need a consumer explainer appended to it. A TikTok demo does not need to mention the protocol spec. Each audience has its own language, channels, and proof points. Mixing them dilutes all of them.
