## Allocation structure (recommended split)

| Bucket | % of monthly | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| X post boosts | 50% | Boost the top organic X post each week. Floor: only boost posts with >2x baseline organic engagement. |
| Newsletter tooling | 15% | ESP cost (see [08-NEWSLETTER.md](./08-NEWSLETTER.md)). |
| Tools and stack | 15% | Analytics, scheduling once we automate, design tools shared with Creative. |
| Partner and influencer | 15% | Paid placements in builder-facing newsletters (Milk Road Pro, Bankless dev list, agent-framework newsletters when applicable). Ops handles outreach; budget is owned here. |
| Reserve | 5% | Opportunistic spend - a partner moment, a conference week, an earned-media boost. |

## Caps and rules

- **Monthly cap:** to be set by `[client]`. No spend above the cap without explicit approval.
- **Per-campaign cap:** no single campaign over 40% of monthly cap without `[client]` sign-off. See [05-CAMPAIGNS.md](./05-CAMPAIGNS.md).
- **Boost floor:** only boost organic posts that have already cleared 2x baseline engagement. Boosting weak posts is wasted spend.
- **ROI threshold:** track cost-per-follower and cost-per-email-signup per campaign. If a campaign comes in 3x more expensive than baseline organic for 2 consecutive months, pause and re-evaluate.
- **Kill switch:** if total paid spend is producing fewer than 1 follower per $5 or 1 email per $10 across a full month, pause paid and rework before continuing.

## What we don't spend on

- Generic crypto-Twitter shilling networks
- Bought followers, bought engagement, bought email lists
- Display ads on general crypto sites
- Paid promotion that doesn't tie to a named post, campaign, or audience
